Looking from the outside, everything was fine - I trained, traveled, I was healthy, full of energy, beautiful, happy and with a smile on my face, but that was not the whole truth about me. All this time I struggled with eating problems. They were not visible on the outside (I even worked as a model for a while), but internally, I felt trapped in food. And although I heard many compliments about my appearance, I knew it was not true and I felt disgusted with myself. I still wanted to be slimmer, I followed various diets and at the same time, it happened that I devoured huge amounts of food, so that I could not move and breathe. Later, I did not eat for a few days and burned calories in the gym by running for 3-4 hours. It was my penance for indulging in self-indulgence. Until the next heist. Ashamed, no one knew about my problem for a long time. It was only years later that I started looking for help. First by reading countless handbooks, then at psychotherapists. In Italy, I participated in individual and group therapy with various therapists. Later I underwent CBT (cognitive - behavioral) therapy in London and attended a therapy group. The effects, however, did not last too long. All the time I had the feeling that something was missing or there was something wrong with me. It was only training at the Eating Psychology Institute in Colorado, USA, the world's leading school of nutritional psychology, that allowed me to look at my problem more broadly. I slowly started putting my affairs in order. Today I am sharing my experience with others.

Eating Psychology Coach is a certificate awarded by the Institute for the Psychology of Eating in Colorado, USA.
